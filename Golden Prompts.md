
Prompt to convert a Research Paper into Slides:
I am uploading a research paper in PDF format.
Your task is to act as a world-class presentation strategist, research analyst, and slide communication expert.
Read the full paper carefully. Understand not just what the authors said, but what matters most to the audience.
Then transform the paper into a clear, visually strong, executive-level 10-slide presentation.
Core Objective
Do NOT summarize the paper section by section.
Instead, distill the paper into a presentation that helps a smart but busy audience grasp:
What problem this paper solves
Why it matters
What was discovered
Whether the findings are credible
What decisions or actions should follow
Audience
Assume the audience is intelligent, time-constrained professionals who want clarity fast.
Step 1: Think Before Writing Slides
First identify:
Central research question
Why this topic matters now
Core argument or hypothesis
Method used
Strongest evidence
Most important findings
Surprising insights
Real-world implications
Risks / limitations
Final takeaway
Then decide what deserves attention and what can be ignored.
Step 2: Create a Strong Narrative Arc
Use this storyline:
Title + Why This Paper Matters
The Problem / Opportunity
What the Researchers Wanted to Know
How the Study Was Done
Key Insight #1
Key Insight #2
Key Insight #3
What This Means in Practice
Limitations / What to Be Careful About
Final Takeaways / Recommended Actions
Step 3: Slide Communication Rules
For every slide:
Use a sharp takeaway headline, not a topic label
One core message per slide
Minimal text
Use plain language
Convert complexity into visuals
Prefer charts, frameworks, timelines, comparisons, process diagrams, matrices
Highlight numbers that matter
Make each slide understandable in 5 seconds
Step 4: Design Standard
Use a premium consulting / keynote presentation style:
Clean layout
Strong hierarchy
Professional typography
White space
Modern visuals
16:9 widescreen
No clutter
No academic-looking dense slides
Step 5: Output Format
For each slide provide:
Slide X
Title:
Core takeaway:
Recommended visual:
Slide text:
Speaker note (1–2 lines)
Step 6: Final Quality Filter
Before finalizing, check:
Does the deck flow logically?
Are weak details removed?
Are strongest insights emphasized?
Would a senior executive stay engaged?
Does this feel designed, not dumped?
If needed, improve before presenting final output.
